Unsupervised Anomaly Detection for Traffic Surveillance.

This work is for NVIDIA AI CITY CHALLENGE track-2, traffic anomaly detection in surveillance video. We figured out the nature among all broken vehicles, that is whenever an anomaly happens, it will lead to at least one stopped vehicle, which becomes part of the video background. According to this finding, we designed a well frame and ranks 2nd in the final competition.

view more on Github.

Also, you can find the demo video here, and paper is here .

  • Designed a novel unsupervised system to detection vehicle anomalies in traffic surveillance videos that could adapt to various scenes without special treatment
  • Implemented Faster-RCNN to detect vehicles in multi-scaled background frames which were captured by MOG2, and leveraged VGG as a powerful classifier to eliminate false detected bounding boxes
  • Designed a decision module to determine anomalous vehicles based on bounding boxes and used ResNet trained with triplet loss to complete similarity comparison when meeting camera-movement or vehicles waiting for traffic light
  • Achieved 0.81 F1-score and 10.2 RMSE in traffic anomaly detection on NVIDIA AI CITY Challenge track-2 dataset

Denoising for Cosmic Microwave Background(CMB) signal with Auto-Encoders.

In this project, my target is to remove tough noise on the data, which is called 'all'. Using the approach of cross-connect, the data of noise is obtained from the first autoencoder whose input is the 'all'. Sequentially, the gap between the 'all' and the 'noise' is the input of next autoencoder. A fantastic result has been acquired from the stack of autoencoders.

view more on Github

  • Cosidering about the characteristic of these data, I decided to cross-connect the layers nearby the input and the layers nearby the output to get more informations as I hoped.
  • Due to that the gap between the 'all' and the 'noise' gotten from first autoencoder is so similar with the real cmb that i decide to put less convolution layers in the second autpencoder.
  • Respectively, the mean absolute error(mae) and mean square error are 8 and 450.
